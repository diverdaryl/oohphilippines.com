How to Book a Billboard in the Philippines: A Step-by-Step Guide

From defining your objectives to watching your creative go up — everything you need to know to book your first (or next) billboard campaign.

Booking a billboard in the Philippines is straightforward once you know the process — but for first-time advertisers, the landscape can feel fragmented. Dozens of operators, no centralized pricing, and a booking process that still runs largely on phone calls and email chains. This guide walks you through every step from campaign brief to creative installation so you know exactly what to expect.

1
Define Your Campaign Objective

Before you look at a single site, be clear on what you want your billboard campaign to achieve. This shapes every decision that follows — location, format, creative approach, and budget allocation.

The most common OOH campaign objectives in the Philippines are:

  • Brand awareness: Getting your name, product, or service in front of as many people as possible. Prioritizes high-traffic locations and large formats.
  • Geographic targeting: Reaching a specific city, district, or corridor — useful for store openings, local service businesses, or regional launches.
  • Audience targeting: Reaching a specific demographic segment — commuters, shoppers, business professionals — based on where they move through the city.
  • Event or campaign support: Amplifying a specific launch, promo, or activation with time-bound OOH visibility.
  • Competitive presence: Matching or countering a competitor's OOH footprint in a key market.

Your objective also determines your success metrics — whether that's estimated impressions, share of voice in a corridor, or simply visual presence during a campaign window.

2
Set Your Budget

Billboard advertising in the Philippines ranges widely in cost — from PHP 20,000/month for a lamp post banner series to over PHP 1,200,000/month for a premium EDSA landmark site. Having a realistic budget range before you start your search saves significant time.

A few things your budget needs to cover beyond the site rental rate:

  • Production: Printing and fabricating the vinyl or tarpaulin creative (typically PHP 5,000–30,000+ depending on size)
  • Installation: Labor for mounting the creative on the structure (often included by the operator, but confirm)
  • Agency fees: If you're working through a media agency, factor in their commission or service fee (typically 15–20% of media spend)
  • VAT: Billboard rates in the Philippines are typically quoted exclusive of the 12% VAT
Budget Tip

For a brand's first Metro Manila OOH campaign, a working budget of PHP 300,000–600,000/month (inclusive of production) typically secures 2–4 well-placed static sites on secondary corridors, or 1 premium EDSA placement. Scale up from there as you validate the channel.

3
Choose Your Market and Location

The Philippines has distinct OOH markets, each with its own audience profile, traffic patterns, and rate levels. Choosing the right market is as important as choosing the right site within that market.

  • Metro Manila — the largest and most competitive OOH market. EDSA, C5, Commonwealth, and SLEX/NLEX are the primary corridors. Highest rates, highest impressions.
  • Cebu — the country's second-largest OOH market. SRP, Mandaue, Airport Road, and IT Park are the key areas. Rates are 30–50% lower than Metro Manila for comparable formats.
  • Davao — a growing market with strong commercial activity along Quimpo Boulevard, Diversion Road, and Lanang.
  • Iloilo, Bacolod, CDO, Baguio — active provincial markets with localized operators and accessible rates.

Within your chosen market, think about corridor vs. destination. Corridor sites (along EDSA, C5, highways) deliver mass reach to moving commuters. Destination sites (near malls, CBDs, intersections) deliver targeted reach to people at or near a specific place.

Location Strategy

If you're launching in a new market, start with 1–2 high-traffic corridor sites for reach, then add a destination site near your store or key retail channel to drive foot traffic. This combination covers both brand and activation objectives.

4
Select Your Format

The format you choose affects your cost, creative requirements, and the kind of impression you make. The main OOH formats available in the Philippines are:

  • Static billboard (vinyl/tarpaulin): The most common format. 100% share of voice, your creative displayed 24/7 for the full campaign period. Best for brand awareness and simple, bold messaging.
  • LED / Digital screen (DOOH): Shared rotation among multiple advertisers (typically 8–15 second loops). Lower cost than a static exclusive, with the ability to update creative during the campaign. Best for promotions, time-sensitive offers, or multi-message campaigns.
  • Lamp post banners: Double-sided panels along road medians, booked in series. Creates a corridor domination effect at a lower cost entry point. Great for events and launches.
  • Transit / bus ads: Full wraps, side panels, or rear panels on buses. Your brand moves through the city along major routes, adding reach to your fixed billboard placements.
Creative Note

Billboard creative works best when it communicates in 3 seconds or less — one strong visual, one clear message, one call to action. The more complex your message, the harder it is to execute effectively in OOH format. Keep it simple, bold, and readable from 50+ meters.

5
Find Available Sites

This is traditionally the most time-consuming part of the billboard booking process in the Philippines. The OOH market is highly fragmented — hundreds of sites spread across dozens of operators, with no centralized inventory system. Finding out what's available has historically meant contacting operators one by one and waiting for availability reports.

There are two main ways to find sites:

  • Direct operator outreach: Contact billboard operators directly via their websites, email, or phone. You'll need to know which operators are active in your target area, reach out individually, and consolidate responses yourself. Time-intensive but gives you direct operator relationships.
  • OOH directory platforms: Use a platform like OOHPhilippines.com to search aggregated inventory from multiple operators in one place, filter by location and format, and contact operators directly from the listing.

6
Request Rate Cards and Availability

Once you've identified sites that interest you, contact the operators to confirm two things: the current rate and availability for your campaign dates. Don't assume a listed rate is current — rates can change, and popular sites may already be booked by the time you inquire.

When reaching out to an operator, include:

  • The specific site(s) you're interested in (site name or location)
  • Your desired campaign period (start and end date, or duration)
  • Your format preference if the site has multiple options
  • Whether you're a direct advertiser or an agency (some operators have different rate structures)

Most operators respond within 1–3 business days with a formal rate card and availability confirmation. For high-demand sites on EDSA or key corridors, availability moves fast — if a site is available and fits your plan, move quickly to hold it.

Negotiation Tip

For multi-site bookings with the same operator, or for campaigns of 3 months or longer, there is usually room to negotiate. Operators generally prefer longer commitments and will often offer better rates for volume or extended duration. Always ask.

7
Evaluate and Shortlist Your Sites

With rate cards and availability confirmed, evaluate your shortlisted sites against your campaign objectives. Key factors to consider:

  • Visibility: Is the site unobstructed? Are there trees, overpasses, or competing structures that may block sightlines? Ideally, do a physical site visit or review a Google Street View of the location.
  • Direction: Does the billboard face the traffic flow you want to reach? A northbound-facing billboard on EDSA captures evening homebound traffic — check whether that aligns with your audience and message.
  • Proximity to your target: Is the site near your store, key retailer, or target audience's daily route?
  • Value per impression: Compare rate vs. estimated traffic volume. A site at PHP 300,000/month in a high-traffic area may deliver better value than a PHP 150,000/month site with half the impressions.
  • Permit status: Confirm the site is fully permitted by the relevant LGU. A reputable operator will have this in order, but it's worth asking explicitly.
8
Sign the Contract and Issue Payment

Once you've confirmed your sites, the operator will issue a booking order or contract outlining the site details, campaign period, rate, payment terms, and creative specifications. Review this carefully before signing.

Key things to check in the contract:

  • Campaign dates: Confirm the exact start and end date, and whether the period includes installation days.
  • Payment schedule: Most operators require payment before installation. Some require full payment upfront; others accept 50% upon booking and 50% before go-live.
  • Production responsibility: Clarify whether the operator prints and installs, or whether you deliver the printed material.
  • Cancellation and force majeure clauses: Understand the terms if you need to cancel or if the site becomes unavailable (e.g. due to structural issues or LGU orders).
  • Proof of posting: Confirm the operator will provide photos or documentation once your creative is installed.
Payment Note

Always pay through official business channels and request an official receipt (OR). Avoid informal arrangements or personal transfers for significant ad spend — even for smaller operators. A signed booking order protects both parties.

9
Prepare and Submit Your Artwork

Once the booking is confirmed, the operator will provide artwork specifications — the required file format, dimensions, resolution, and submission deadline. Most static billboard operators require:

  • File format: High-resolution PDF, AI (Adobe Illustrator), or TIFF
  • Resolution: Typically 25–72 DPI at actual size (large format prints at lower DPI than standard print)
  • Color mode: CMYK for print production
  • Bleed: Usually 6–12 inches on all sides beyond the live area
  • Submission deadline: Typically 5–7 business days before the campaign start date to allow for printing and installation

For LED/DOOH screens, the operator will provide specific pixel dimensions, file format (usually MP4 or JPEG), and the duration of your creative slot.

Creative Tip

Always request a site template from your operator — a file with the exact dimensions and safe zones for your specific billboard structure. Designing to a template prevents creative elements from being cut off or falling outside the visible area after installation.

10
Installation and Campaign Go-Live

The operator's production team handles printing (if they are responsible for production) and installation. For large static billboards, installation typically takes half a day to a full day depending on the structure height and location. LED campaigns go live as soon as the creative file is uploaded to the screen management system — often the same day artwork is approved.

A few things to do around the go-live date:

  • Visit the site: Do a physical inspection once your creative is up. Check visibility from the road, confirm the creative is properly mounted with no wrinkles or misalignment, and validate that illumination is working for night visibility.
  • Document it: Take your own photos and videos — both during the day and at night — to use for internal reporting, social media, and campaign records.
  • Share it: A photo of your billboard going live is strong social media content and a tangible signal of brand credibility for stakeholders and partners.
11
Proof of Posting and Monitoring

Once your campaign is live, reputable operators will provide proof of posting (POP) — photographs taken of the installed creative from the road, confirming it is up and in good condition. Request this if the operator doesn't send it automatically.

For the duration of your campaign, periodically monitor your sites — especially for static billboards that can be affected by weather, vandalism, or structural issues. Most operators will repair or replace damaged creative at no cost during an active campaign, but you need to report the issue promptly.

At the end of your campaign, evaluate performance against your original objectives:

  • Did you achieve the visibility and presence you intended?
  • Did the location deliver the audience reach you expected?
  • Were there any execution issues (creative quality, installation timing, site condition) worth noting for future campaigns?

OOH campaigns are most powerful when they're part of an ongoing brand presence strategy — not a one-off. Use your first campaign as a baseline to refine location selection, creative approach, and operator relationships for the next one.
