The Philippine OOH Landscape

The Philippines has one of the most active out-of-home (OOH) advertising markets in Southeast Asia. Driven by dense urban populations, high commuter volumes, and a culture that responds strongly to visual media, billboard advertising remains a core channel for brands — from major multinationals down to regional businesses building local presence.

The market is served by a mix of large national operators with hundreds of sites across multiple cities, and smaller regional specialists that dominate specific corridors or markets. Understanding who the key players are — and what each one brings to the table — is the first step to building a smarter OOH media plan.

The companies listed here are drawn from OOHPhilippines.com's live billboard inventory — operators with verified listings across Metro Manila, Cebu, Davao, and the provinces.

1. United Neon Media Group

1
United Neon Media Group
The Philippines' largest OOH operator by inventory
National Static & LED
United Neon Media Group (UNMG) is the most dominant billboard operator in the Philippines by sheer volume of inventory. With over 220 verified sites in the OOHPhilippines.com directory alone — and a total portfolio that spans the entire country — they are the go-to partner for advertisers seeking national OOH reach under a single operator relationship. Their presence on EDSA is particularly strong, with sites spread across virtually every major section from Caloocan to Magallanes.
Beyond Metro Manila, United Neon operates in Cebu, Davao, Iloilo, and key provincial corridors — making them one of the few operators capable of executing a truly nationwide billboard campaign. Their portfolio covers both static and illuminated formats, with a growing footprint in LED and digital screens.

Outcomm
Premium static and digital OOH across Metro Manila

3. ECO Bridges

3
ECO Bridges
Large-scale bridge and flyover advertising specialist
Specialist Metro Manila
ECO Bridges holds one of the largest OOH portfolios in Metro Manila with 75+ sites in our directory — concentrated heavily on bridges, flyovers, and elevated road structures across the city. This niche gives ECO Bridges a distinctive positioning: their sites are seen by motorists at elevated vantage points, often with unobstructed sightlines that standard roadside billboards can't match.
For brands looking to dominate a specific interchange or highway overpass — particularly in the EDSA-Balintawak to C5 corridor — ECO Bridges is a key operator to engage. Their inventory is particularly strong for advertisers targeting motorists and commuters on major Metro Manila road networks.

How to Find and Compare These Operators

Knowing who the operators are is only half the equation. The harder part — traditionally — has been finding out which of their specific sites are available, at what rates, and in which exact locations. Most advertisers and agencies have had to rely on cold calls, email chains, and waiting days for availability reports that were often outdated by the time they arrived.

OOHPhilippines.com was built to solve this. Our directory aggregates verified inventory from all the operators listed here — plus dozens more regional operators across the Philippines — so you can search by location, format, and operator in one place, and connect directly with the right contact when you're ready to book.

Media Planner Tip

When building a Metro Manila OOH plan, a common approach is to anchor with a United Neon or Outcomm site on EDSA for reach, then supplement with 2–3 sites from mid-size operators on secondary corridors (C5, EDSA-Shaw, Commonwealth) for frequency. This gives you broad coverage without putting the entire budget into a single operator.
