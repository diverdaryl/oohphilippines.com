What Is a Billboard Directory?

A billboard directory is a centralized database of outdoor advertising sites — organized by location, format, operator, and availability — that lets advertisers search and compare inventory without having to contact each operator individually.

Think of it the way you'd think of a property listing site for real estate, or a hotel booking platform for travel. Instead of calling every landlord or hotel directly, you browse a single platform that aggregates the inventory, filters it by what matters to you, and connects you with the right contact when you're ready to transact.

In the Philippine OOH market — which has historically been fragmented, relationship-driven, and hard to navigate without industry contacts — a billboard directory removes the biggest barrier for advertisers: knowing what's out there and how to reach the right operator.

Who Uses the OOHPhilippines.com Directory

Advertising Agencies & Media Planners

Agencies use the directory to source sites quickly during the planning phase — especially for unfamiliar markets or when building multi-city campaigns that would otherwise require separate operator outreach per region. Brands going direct to operators — bypassing an agency — use the directory to understand what's available, at what locations, and who to call. Local businesses in Cebu, Davao, Iloilo, and other provincial markets use the directory to find operators in their own city who they may not have known existed. How to Use the Directory

Using the OOHPhilippines.com billboard directory is straightforward:

  1. Browse or search by location. Start with a city or corridor — Metro Manila, EDSA, Cebu, Davao, or any of the city pages. The directory filters listings by location automatically.
  2. Review the listing details. Each site shows location, size, format, illumination, and operator contact. Click through to see the Street View of the site from the road.
  3. Shortlist your preferred sites. Identify 3–5 sites that match your campaign criteria — location, format, direction, and estimated traffic volume.
  4. Contact the operator directly. Use the email, phone, Viber, or WhatsApp contact on the listing to inquire about availability and rates for your campaign dates.
  5. Book and go live. Once availability is confirmed and rates are agreed, follow the standard billboard booking process to get your campaign live.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is OOHPhilippines.com free to use?

Yes — browsing the billboard directory is free for advertisers, agencies, and media planners. You can search listings, preview sites, and access operator contact details at no cost.

Are the listings up to date?

The directory is continuously updated as operators add new sites and update availability. Because billboard inventory changes frequently — sites get booked, campaigns end, new structures are built — always confirm current availability directly with the operator before finalizing your media plan.

Can I book a billboard directly through the platform?

The directory connects you directly with operators — booking is completed through your agreement with the operator, not through an intermediary. This keeps the process transparent and gives you a direct relationship with the company managing your site.

I'm an operator. How do I list my sites?

Billboard operators can list their inventory on OOHPhilippines.com by creating an operator account. Listed sites are visible to all advertisers and agencies searching the directory. Get started here →

Does the directory cover formats other than billboards?

Yes — the directory includes static billboards, LED and digital screens, lamp post banners, and other OOH formats. Coverage varies by city and operator.
